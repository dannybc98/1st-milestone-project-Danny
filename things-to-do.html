Beaches

While the island is not known for its typical sandy shores, it boasts several pebble and rock beaches, each with its own distinct charm. Praia Formosa, with its striking seafront promenade, invites relaxation and captivating sunsets. Praia da Calheta, with its golden sand imported from Morocco, offers a more traditional beach experience, complete with clear waters perfect for swimming. Hiking

Madeira's hiking experience is a journey into breathtaking landscapes and natural wonders. The island's extensive network of levada walks and mountain trails caters to all levels of hikers, from leisurely strolls to challenging treks. As you venture along the ancient levadas, surrounded by lush forests and cascading waterfalls, you'll be captivated by the serenity of the surroundings. Hiking up the peaks, like Pico do Arieiro, rewards you with awe-inspiring vistas of rugged terrain and vast ocean panoramas. The diverse flora and fauna, along with the fresh mountain air, invigorate the senses, creating an unforgettable and rejuvenating outdoor experience for nature enthusiasts and adventure seekers alike.

Whale & Dolphin watching

Whale and dolphin watching in Madeira Island offers an enchanting experience. Boat tours bring you up close to playful dolphins and majestic whales in the azure waters of the Atlantic. Expert guides provide insights, making it an unforgettable adventure that deepens your appreciation for marine life and the wonders of the natural world.

Madeira wine tasting

Madeira wine's unique production process involves oxidative aging, imparting it with exceptional longevity and character. The experience is not only a celebration of the senses but also a tribute to Madeira's rich cultural heritage, rooted in vineyards that cling to terraced hillsides and continue to produce wines cherished worldwide.
